# Hexagonal Lattice

The **hexagonal lattice** is one of the five fundamental two-dimensional Bravais lattices. It is characterized by a regular hexagonal symmetry, consisting of lattice points forming equilateral triangles.

## Basic Definitions

The hexagonal lattice can be fully described by two primitive lattice vectors $\mathbf{a}_1$ and $\mathbf{a}_2$ with equal magnitudes and an angle of 120° between them.

$$
|\mathbf{a}_1| = |\mathbf{a}_2| = a, \quad \mathbf{a}_1 \cdot \mathbf{a}_2 = a^2 \cos(120^\circ) = -\frac{a^2}{2}
$$

## Lattice Vectors and Primitive Cell

The primitive vectors can be explicitly written as:

$$
\mathbf{a}_1 = a \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \mathbf{a}_2 = a \begin{pmatrix} -\frac{1}{2} \\ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \end{pmatrix}
$$

### Primitive Cell Area

The area of the primitive unit cell for the hexagonal lattice is:

$$
A = |\mathbf{a}_1 \times \mathbf{a}_2| = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}a^2 \approx 0.866a^2
$$

## Reciprocal Lattice

The reciprocal lattice vectors $\mathbf{b}_1$ and $\mathbf{b}_2$ satisfy the conditions:

$$
\mathbf{a}_i \cdot \mathbf{b}_j = 2\pi \delta_{ij}
$$

Explicitly, the reciprocal vectors are:

$$
\mathbf{b}_1 = \frac{2\pi}{a}\begin{pmatrix}1 \\ \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\end{pmatrix}, \quad \mathbf{b}_2 = \frac{2\pi}{a}\begin{pmatrix}0 \\ \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}\end{pmatrix}
$$

### Reciprocal Lattice Area

The area of the reciprocal lattice primitive cell is:

$$
A^* = |\mathbf{b}_1 \times \mathbf{b}_2| = \frac{8\pi^2}{\sqrt{3}a^2} \approx 4.618\frac{\pi^2}{a^2}
$$

## Key Takeaways

* The hexagonal lattice is highly symmetric with sixfold rotational symmetry.
* It serves as the foundation for important physical systems, such as graphene and other two-dimensional materials.
* Both direct and reciprocal lattices exhibit hexagonal symmetry.
