# 2D Bravais Lattices

The five 2D Bravais lattices represent all possible ways to arrange points periodically in a plane while maintaining translational symmetry. Each lattice is characterized by its lattice parameters (lengths and angles) and symmetry properties.

## Overview of the Five 2D Bravais Lattices

### 1. [Oblique Lattice](./oblique)
- **Most general case** with no constraints
- **Parameters**: a ≠ b (generally), γ ≠ 90°, 120°
- **Point Group**: p1 (minimal symmetry)
- **Coordination Number**: 4

### 2. [Rectangular Lattice](./rectangular)
- **Perpendicular vectors** of different lengths
- **Parameters**: a ≠ b, γ = 90°
- **Point Group**: p2mm
- **Coordination Number**: 4

### 3. [Centered Rectangular Lattice](./centered_rectangular)
- **Rectangular with centering** 
- **Parameters**: a ≠ b, γ = 90°, plus center points
- **Point Group**: c2mm
- **Coordination Number**: 4

### 4. [Square Lattice](./square)
- **Equal perpendicular vectors**
- **Parameters**: a = b, γ = 90°
- **Point Group**: p4mm (four-fold symmetry)
- **Coordination Number**: 4

### 5. [Hexagonal Lattice](./hexagonal)
- **Highest symmetry and optimal packing**
- **Parameters**: a = b, γ = 120°
- **Point Group**: p6mm (six-fold symmetry)
- **Coordination Number**: 6

## Classification by Symmetry

The 2D Bravais lattices can be organized by increasing symmetry:

```
Oblique → Rectangular → Square
              ↓           ↑
    Centered Rectangular  ↑
              ↓           ↑
           Hexagonal ----→
```

## Key Properties Comparison

| Lattice Type | Parameters | Point Group | Coordination | Max Packing |
|--------------|------------|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| Oblique | a, b, γ arbitrary | p1 | 4 | Variable |
| Rectangular | a ≠ b, γ = 90° | p2mm | 4 | ~1.11 |
| Centered Rect. | a ≠ b, γ = 90°, centered | c2mm | 4 | Variable |
| Square | a = b, γ = 90° | p4mm | 4 | π/4 ≈ 0.785 |
| Hexagonal | a = b, γ = 120° | p6mm | 6 | π/(2√3) ≈ 0.907 |

## Mathematical Framework

### Lattice Vectors
Each 2D lattice is defined by two primitive vectors **a₁** and **a₂**:
- **Lattice points**: R = n₁**a₁** + n₂**a₂** (n₁, n₂ ∈ ℤ)
- **Unit cell area**: A = |**a₁** × **a₂**|

### Reciprocal Lattice
The reciprocal lattice vectors are:
- **b₁** = 2π(**a₂** × ẑ)/(**a₁** · (**a₂** × ẑ))
- **b₂** = 2π(ẑ × **a₁**)/(**a₁** · (**a₂** × ẑ))

### Metric Tensor
The metric tensor G describes distances and angles:
```
G = [a₁·a₁  a₁·a₂]
    [a₁·a₂  a₂·a₂]
```

## Historical Context

### Development Timeline
- **1848**: Auguste Bravais identifies the 14 3D Bravais lattices
- **1879**: Leonhard Sohncke extends to 2D cases
- **1891**: Fedorov, Schoenflies, Barlow: Complete space group classification
- **1912**: Max von Laue: First X-ray diffraction experiments
- **1920s-30s**: Quantum mechanics applied to crystal electrons

### Modern Applications
- **1985**: Discovery of fullerenes and carbon lattices
- **2004**: Isolation of graphene (hexagonal/honeycomb)
- **2010s**: 2D materials revolution (TMDCs, etc.)
- **2020s**: Moiré engineering and twisted bilayers

## Future Directions

- **Moiré lattices**: Superposition of 2D lattices
